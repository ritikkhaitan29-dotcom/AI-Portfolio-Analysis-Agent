# Portfolio Analysis Methodology

## Overview

The AI Portfolio Analysis Agent follows a structured framework for evaluating investment portfolios using publicly available information and user-provided portfolio holdings.

Rather than analysing each investment independently, the workflow evaluates how the holdings interact as a portfolio to assess diversification, concentration risk, portfolio quality and long-term investment suitability.

Every portfolio is assessed using the same analytical framework to improve consistency and comparability.

---

## Portfolio Analysis Workflow

```text
          Portfolio Holdings
                  │
                  ▼
        Understand Portfolio
                  │
                  ▼
     Asset Allocation Analysis
                  │
                  ▼
 Diversification & Concentration
                  │
                  ▼
      Sector & Industry Exposure
                  │
                  ▼
 Portfolio Quality Assessment
                  │
                  ▼
   Risk & Scenario Analysis
                  │
                  ▼
 Portfolio Recommendations
```

---

## Stage 1 — Understand the Portfolio

The workflow begins by identifying the composition of the portfolio.

This includes:

- Number of holdings
- Position sizes
- Asset classes
- Investment objectives
- Investment horizon

---

## Stage 2 — Evaluate Allocation

The portfolio is assessed to determine whether capital is allocated efficiently across holdings.

The analysis reviews:

- Position sizing
- Concentration
- Sector allocation
- Geographic allocation (where applicable)

---

## Stage 3 — Assess Portfolio Quality

Each holding contributes to the overall quality assessment of the portfolio.

Rather than focusing solely on expected returns, the framework considers:

- Business quality
- Financial strength
- Competitive advantages
- Governance
- Earnings quality
- Valuation

---

## Stage 4 — Risk Assessment

The workflow identifies risks that may not be apparent when analysing individual companies.

Examples include:

- Excessive concentration
- Correlated holdings
- Sector dependency
- Style concentration
- Valuation risk

---

## Stage 5 — Portfolio Recommendations

The final stage converts observations into practical actions.

Recommendations may include:

- Reduce concentration
- Improve diversification
- Rebalance allocations
- Replace weaker holdings
- Monitor key portfolio risks

---

## Guiding Principles

The methodology is built around several core principles.

- Capital preservation before return maximisation.
- Portfolio quality over portfolio size.
- Evidence-based analysis.
- Consistency across every portfolio review.
- AI supports investment judgement rather than replacing it.
